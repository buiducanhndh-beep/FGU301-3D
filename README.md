# FGU301-3D
Game concept: "Bridge exploration"
Overview: 3D Game
Genre: character, exploration
Platform: PC, Self-exploit Asset store to have different components. Not accepted if 2 posts are similar to 50% or more of the components in the game.
Gameplay mechanism:
1. Character (20 points):
•	Players control a bridge builder to go through the pillars. Each pillar will have 1 random gift.
•	Control the character to go straight, turn left and right.
•	Combine speed control according to the tilt for an immersive experience.
2. Bridge mission (30 points): Players stand at a bridge pillar. The next bridge pillar can be fixed or move closer or further away from the bridge pillar the player is standing on. The height of the next bridge pillar can also be fixed (equal, higher or lower) or change the height up and down within a range.
When the player presses the Space key (If the pillars are randomly changing distance and height, they will have to stop for the player to estimate) the bridge is erected vertically upwards with the length of the bridge corresponding to the time the space key is held, the width of the bridge is fixed = 2*5 the size of the player's feet. The player uses the option keys to adjust the character's view direction. After adjusting the view direction to the next bridge pillar, press the (Optional) key and the bridge will fall down in the direction the character is looking. If the bridge head is on the right bridge pillar, the player will then control the character using the movement fees to go to the other bridge pillar and receive a gift. If the bridge head is longer or shorter, the player will lose (when losing, you can exchange gifts or points to play again, otherwise you will return to the starting pillar)
3. Unity's physics tool: Slope Physics (10 points):
•	Use Unity's physics tool to have realistic walking movements. If you are going straight across the bridge and press the left or right arrow, you will fall.
•	Implement friction, gravity based on the slope of the bridge.
4. Overcoming obstacles and challenges (10 points):
•	When the player walks across the next bridge pillar. Design levels with various obstacles on the bridge that the player must jump or crouch to avoid.
•	Include items to gain extra points such as coins or flowers like mario.
5. User interface design (10 points):
•	Main menu with Start, Options and Exit buttons.
•	In-game UI displays current score, Level, current bridge pillar position and player's life.
6. Handling collisions and triggers (10 points):
•	Implement collisions with obstacles that affect the player's speed or fall off the bridge.
•	Use triggers to increase power (speed up, invincibility) or shortcuts by combining gifts or points.
7. Game Logic and Scoring System (10 points):
•	Score based on speed, items collected, and tricks like jumping, crouching, and even successful shooting.
•	Incorporate a combo system to connect tricks together for x3 points.
•	Implement a x2 multiplier for consecutive successful moves.
•	Combine items, gifts, and points for power-ups.
